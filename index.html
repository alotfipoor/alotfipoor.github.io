About Me

My name is Ashkan Lotfipoor. I’m an Engineering Geology graduate from the University of Tehran who works as a Data Analyst for Samsung electronics in Iran. Currently, I’m looking for opportunities to continue my education as a PhD student.

For my master thesis, I used ML algorithms to develop a new method for estimating the impacts of tunneling on springs discharge rate. At the same time I worked at Samsung Electronics as a Data Analyst. During this period I found a new appreciation for using data to solve complex problems. For this reasons, I’m eager to learn more in this path and to use the techniques that I’ve learned to uncover new grounds in rock mechanics.

Thus, my research interests lie primarily in an area between Engineering Geology and Data Analysis. There are many problems that can be tackled with this new approach, such as various soil and rock properties (e.g. ground bearing capacity estimation), environmental impact assessment of projects, performance predictions of TBM, slope stability using historical data of similar failure and so on. So, right now my research interest is to combine my growing knowledge of Data Science and Engineering Geology to solve complex problems by mining and modeling the data streams from different sources.
